Her group has also made relevant contributions to the understanding of: (i) The CoV transcription mechanism, leading to the current transcription model; (ii) CoV-host interactions, leading to the identification of viral virulence genes and innate immune cell pathways relevant for pathogenesis; (iii) The relevance of small non-coding RNAs in the regulation of CoV-host interactions, leading to the identification for the first time in CoVs of small viral RNAs that contribute to the SARS-CoV induced lung inflammatory pathology.
